September 2022

Akshita Bawa

This month went by really quickly but here are some things I would like to highlight:

  • New ITP landing page for prospective students!! This is the main project I have been working on and will be working on during the entire residency. The project has been a WIP for the last one year and we are taking it forward to roll out in Spring 2022!! This could have not been possible without the incredible Siteseers team and I am so grateful for Elias and Yonatan to join the team and help things move forward.
  • I have also been helping out with the Design Lab workshops and recently hosted the one on Figma. Eden and Michael have been a great support with the whole process. New hires for Design Lan are here too, really excited to have them on board.
  • In collaboration with Design Lab and with immense help and support from Eden, Vesper and Emma, I am organizing the Inktober Challenge 2022 at ITP. It will be a 2 weeks mini-challenge similar to 100 days format wherein everyone has to create an art piece everyday using ink and in the end we’ll display all the work in a gallery format in the Media Commons. The art pieces can take any form and the ink can be applied using any medium - want to make a flip book? Or use a plotter to create a piece? The list is endless. Look out for the email soon.
  • Sam is building a corset and I am helping her in whatever little ways I can! Garment construction is a tedious process and has been an integral part of my journey as an undergrad. Although I have made different types of garments in the past, this would be my first contribution to a corset.
  • Ami and I recently went to a workshop on Kalamkari by artist Nikita Shah. It was a truly incredible experience and I learned so much about this great art form from India. We are in the process of organising a session for the ITP/IMA Community soon with Nikita. Really excited for this!
  • For my personal exploration and building up on my thesis where I used traditional textile with technology, I plan to do Kalamkari painting using conductive paint to create an installation of sound and light. Hopefully. It is still an idea and a wip but hopefully I can do justice to the art form and tell a story.
  • Also tried Mike’s Hot Honey Iced Latte at Blank Street! Has a spicy aftertaste in the throat. Sam and Alan recommend the hot latte version with Almond Milk!

Eden

Processes and projects that are emerging:

  • Alternative processes for photography, particularly methods that abstract images, particularly figures, in unexpected and even grotesque ways
    • Considering new materials to create prints ie., What would it look like to print photographs on fabric and create a quilt? Why am I interested in this process? What photographs would I use as source material?
    • Hunches: relationship to femininity and domestic labor, relationship to the body and how we care for it, quilts as (family) heirlooms, fractured/discarded swatches that are put back together again to create a beautiful object, something once deemed useless is repurposed and made whole
    • I am investigating this process as a second thesis! That I do alongside my thesis group in a more private way this time around.
  • Onboarded Design Lab Staff! Really excited to be working with Akshita, Michael, and the student DL mentors, Ruhsona, Vesper, Bei, Suraj, and Lifei!
  • Planning courses for outside of the ITP community. Two teaching roles will be kicking off next week and the following!
  • Her Body and Other Parties by Carmen Maria Machado. Highly recommend this book for a spooky and engaging read! It’s an incredible collection of short stories.
  • I’m working with an artist named Billie Allen to plan a solo show of his works. Learn more about ways to support him here.

Sam

  • I’ve been starting to build lots of things! My overall theme for making stuff are “things that I would like to have in my house”, starting with turning my thesis into a dining table. I have a couple of things in the pipeline:
  • I’m making an e-ink clock for my bedroom that will display the time and when the next train comes! So far I’ve realized that MTA uses a funky format for their real time data and it is hard to make that request from an arduino, so I’m doing the backend with python which I have working and setting up a server that sends only the relevant trains to the microcontroller.
  • I’m also building a corset! Akshita and Daniel Ryan have been super helpful in getting this project started! Eden also helped me print out the pattern on the vinyl printer.
  • Phil and I are building a custom keyboard case together. Earlier this week we started modeling the PCB that we have and are creating the 3D model that will be printed.
  • I also have been doing a bunch of research on tarot to inspire a new speculative ritual involving reframed tarot cards (since the Rider-Waite deck is somewhat problematic and rooted in Christian and medieval history).
